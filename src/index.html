Introducing ⇐ Recall, the bookmark search engine

Browsers Aren't Great

Browsers make bookmarks hard.

They can't search through your bookmarks. You have to organise them around themes yourself if you want to be able to find anything later. Browsers save bookmarks to the hard drive of the computer you're on. If you want to see them on another computer later (say if you go home for the day) you're out of luck.

The more bookmarks you have, the harder they are to use. Finding that bookmark from earlier involves having to look through the browser menus for the title you saved it under. You start having to delete bookmarks because you have so many that it's become unmanagable.

Most people have basically given up on using bookmarks. It's too hard. Too fiddly. Too annoying. But it doesn't have to be this way.

Search is Great

Remember when the search engines became popular? Suddenly, the web became useful. Recall is a search engine. It's a bit different through — it's designed to work with bookmarks.

Every time you add a bookmark to Recall, it looks at the web page's text. This text is added to a "search index". Later, when you want to find things again you can do keywords search, just like Google.

Searching makes it much easier to find things. Often, a search will turn up results you had forgotten about or didn't remember were on the same subject.

It's also means that there's a lot less effort in trying to classify and organise things by hand. A lot of time and effort is saved.

Using Recall is Easy

Recall could not be more straightforward to use. Bookmarks are saved with our special browser buttons — "bookmarklets" for most browsers and a Google Chrome extension. Adding Recall to your browser is a snap.

We Don't Sell Your Details

Recall is a purely subscription product. We charge money for our product. This makes us a bit different from traditional "dot-com" businesses. They let anyone sign up for free and make their money by selling their "customers"' data and adverts to marketers.

Recall has proper privacy, not confusing "permissions". When you save a bookmark into Recall you can mark it as private. If it's private, it won't appear to anyone else. It's as simple as that.

We care about your privacy in other ways too. Recall asks for real names by default, but it doesn't insist. You can sign up with a pseudonym (though you probably have to give your real name to Paypal). In the future, we'll look at increasing this kind of pseudonym.

Never Lose Bookmarks Again

Losing data, of any kind, is extremely frustrating.

Your bookmarks are incredibly personal. They represent months, possibly years of reading and research. How would you feel if you lost them?

Most people do no backups whatsoever. Of the few who do, it's doubtful that they rememeber to backup their bookmarks, which are kept inside the browser's program files, not with the rest of the important documents.

Recall will handle the backups for you.

All Together

With web browser bookmarks, often, bookmarks get split up between different computers. This becomes a pain, for example, when you want to look though the bookmarks you made at work when you're in bed with your iPad. It also makes it harder to organise. Did I save a copy of this already on another machine?

Recall keeps everything together. All of your bookmarks are stored in the same pool. So you can search through everything you have no matter where you are.

Your existing bookmarks can be imported into Recall. We accept the "Netscape" bookmark file format, which every major browser can export into. And if you're using something unusual, get in touch and we'll import it as a special job.

No Crafty Adverts

Other search engines' "results" pages are one part results and two parts paid advertisement. We don't take money from marketers. Our search results are always real results — and never crafty adverts designed to look like search results.

We care a lot about our customers. We have every incentive to care, because unlike a lot of big technology companies, it's our customers who pay us. As we've said, we don't sell our customers to advertisers.

Recall, like most software, is not "done". We're going to keep adding improvements and new features and we're going to survey our customers to find out which direction they'd like things to move it.

We really hope that you like it. But if you don't we'll refund your money any time for six months after you subscribe.
