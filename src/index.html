Introducing ⇐ Recall, the bookmark search engine

Browsers Are Bad at Bookmarks

They can't search. You have to organise them yourself if you want to be able to find anything later. Browsers save bookmarks to the hard drive of the computer you're on. If you want to see them on another computer later (say if you go home for the day) you're out of luck.

And the more bookmarks you have, the harder they are to keep organised. Finding that bookmark from earlier means looking through the browser menus for the title you saved it under. Maybe you even start deleting bookmarks because you have so many that organising them is unmanageable.

Most people have given up on using bookmarks. It's too hard. Too fiddly. Too annoying. But it doesn't have to be this way.

All Together

With web browsers, bookmarks often get split up between different computers. This becomes a pain when you want to look though the bookmarks you made at work when you're in bed with your iPad. It also makes it harder to organise. Did I save a copy of this already on another machine? Are my bookmarks all in sync?

Recall keeps everything together. All of your bookmarks are stored in the same pool. You can search through everything you have no matter where you are.

Your existing bookmarks can be imported into Recall. We accept the "Netscape" bookmark file format, which every major browser can export into. And if you're using something unusual, get in touch and we'll import it as a special job.

Never Lose Bookmarks Again

Losing data, of any kind, is extremely frustrating.

Your bookmarks are incredibly personal. They represent months, possibly years of reading and research. How would you feel if you lost them?

Most people do no backups whatsoever. Of the few who do, it's doubtful that they remember to backup their bookmarks, which are kept inside the browser's program files, not with the rest of the important documents.

Recall will handle the backups for you.

Search Is Great

Remember when the first search engines became popular? Suddenly, the web got a lot more useful. Recall is a search engine. It's a bit different though – it's designed to work with bookmarks.

Every time you add a bookmark to Recall, it looks at the web page's text. This text is added to a "search index". Later, when you want to find things again you can do keyword search, just like you can on Google.

Searching makes it much easier to find things. Often, a search will turn up results you had forgotten about or didn't remember were related.

You don't have to bother with doing all the organisation by hand. Just add bookmarks to Recall and then search later.

We Don't Sell Your Details

Recall is a purely subscription product. We charge money! This makes us a bit different from traditional "dot-com" businesses. They let anyone sign up for free but then make their money by selling adverts and customer data on to marketers.

Recall has proper privacy, not confusing "permissions". When you save a bookmark into Recall you can mark it as private. If it's private, no one else can see it. It's as simple as that.

We care about your privacy in other ways too. Recall asks for real names by default, but it doesn't insist. You can use a made up pseudonym instead, though you probably have to give your real name to Paypal.

No Crafty Adverts

Other search engines' results pages are one part results and two parts paid advertisement. We don't take money from marketers, so our search results are always real results. They're never crafty adverts designed to look like search results.

Using Recall Is Easy

Recall could not be more straightforward to use. Bookmarks are saved with our special browser buttons: "bookmarklets" for most browsers and a Google Chrome extension. Adding Recall to your browser is a snap.
